It took us a bit to find our bearings here at Grow the Richmond as we've been adjusting to life under shelter in place. But we're getting the swing of things here. I know for my part, I've been thankful while looking for ways I can continue to stay plugged into my community. That's why I've been volunteering with Together SF, delivering groceries once a week to an elderly neighbor in the Richmond.

However, one of the aspects that's also emerged that I've noticed is that we are not quite as bound by geography so we can bring presenters to events that we might not have imagined or attend events that may have been out of our normal location. YIMBY Action has put together a number of cool events recently highlighting just that.

Without further ado, here's some great events coming up and other ways you can stay engaged with your friends and neighbors in this unprecidented time

What's Happening In Right Now?
  • Mayor London Breed will be joining us at YIMBY Action tonight at 6:30 pm! Check out the details here.
  • Save Our Amazing Richmond (SOAR) is hosting a District 1 candidate forum on May 19th at 6 PM for the upcoming Supervisor race in November. Register here. 
  • On May 21 at 6:30 pm, SF YIMBY will be hosting a candidate night with District 1 Supervisor candidate Marjan Philhour as well as a couple candidates from other districts. More information here.

In local news
  • Have you had a chance to check out the Slow Street on Lake yet? Or a JFK without cars? Heather Knight covers car-free spaces and other silver linings we should keep after the Coronavirus crisis is over.
  • Speaking Lake Street being opened up for more people to enjoy, check out the petition our friends at People Protected put together to Keep Lake Slow 
  • MTA is down to four commissioners. Rachel Swan takes a look at what exactly is going on (full disclosure: I'm a current nominee for one of the open seats)
  • Janelle Bitker looks at the dire state of our local restaurant scene
  • Mayor Breed is proposing a $438 million dollar bond to kickstart our economy

In state and national news
  • The New York Times kicked off a powerful series with The Cities We Need
  • Others in that series include a great piece from Carol Galante of the Terner Center about embracing density, Farhad Manjoo about the precarious and fleeting sense of hope to truly make our region more equitable, and Binyamin Appelbaum on our national indifference to ending homelessness
  • State legislators are considering all sorts of COVID-19 relief, including possible long-term rent relief
  • A conversation about what's in store for Bay Area housing
  • Our State Senator Scott Wiener ably rebuting the troubling talking point of density causing coronavirus

Other events coming up
  • YIMBY Action will be hosting an Intro to YIMBY on May 25th at 7:00 pm. It's a great entry point for anyone looking to get involved!
  • I will be moderating an event with State Senator Scott Wiener about Housing and Health Care in another YIMBY Action event on May 26th at 6 pm
  • Stay tuned for more details about events featuring D1 candidates, including one hosted by San Francisco Young Democrats and the Richmond District Democratic Club as well as one hosted by United Democratic Club and Ed Lee Democratic Club

Take care of yourselves, and of each other.

Until next time, Jane with Grow The Richmond
